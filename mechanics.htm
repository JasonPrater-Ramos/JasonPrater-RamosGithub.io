Mechanics

Spotting - Tanks have a spotting range, which is the range in which they can spot. Factors affect spotting, such as the camo rating of the tank along with if they are hiding behind a bush or tree.

Armor - Armor is a big thing in world of tanks as it can prevent you from taking damage. Either a tank can non-pen the armor or just bounce off. On top of this if the armor is angled, it increases the amount of armor you have. This is known as angling and makes a big difference in the amount of armor you have. Tank specific mechanics

There are certain mechanics that are specific to certain tanks. There are autoloaders, auto reloaders, hydropneumatic suspension, double barrels, gas turbine, and wheel mechanic.

Autoloaders- Tanks with autoloaders, such as the kranvagn or the T57, have something like a magazine of shells inside them. They are able to fire all the shells in their magazine very quickly, but the reload of a magazine takes a while. A smart player will know when to hold shells or reload their magazine.

Autoreloaders- Tanks with autoloaders, such as the rinoceronte or the progetto 65, are very similar to autoloading tanks, but the mechanic works a bit differently. These tanks reload one shell at a time after firing. To best explain it, imagine a shotgun, you can get a shell in the chamber quickly but can still put another shell in right after firing while autoloading tanks are like a machine gun. When playing these tanks, don’t fire all your shots as the further into the magazine you go, the longer the reload takes.

Hydropneumatic suspension- Tanks with this mechanic, such as the STRV 103B or the Udes 15/16, are able to lift the front and back of the tank. This can increase the elevation and depression angles of the tank. Tanks like this should be played as a sniper(STRV) or ambusher(Udes 15/16).

Double barrel- There is only one line of double barrel tanks and can be found in the Russian tech tree. I don’t have one currently but I am working my way up to the tier 8 double barrel tank, the IS-2-II. These tanks should be played similar to the auto reloading tanks, as in you should hold your second shot as it takes longer to reload if you shoot both guns. You can also fire both guns at the same time to do a large amount of damage at once, but it is risky because you will have to wait for the reload and will be vulnerable.

Gas Turbine- As far as I know there is only one tank with a gas turbine mechanic and it is the CS-63. This mechanic gives the tank 2 mobility modes, normal and turbo. Turbo makes the tank go faster but you have a harder time controlling it.This will allow the player to get to key positions faster.

Wheels- There are only two tech trees with wheeled tanks, Britain and France. Britain has wheeled medium tanks while France has wheeled light tanks. These tanks are generally faster and have better mobility. French wheeled tanks have another mechanic where they can use an extra 4 wheels(2 on each side) to increase mobility.
